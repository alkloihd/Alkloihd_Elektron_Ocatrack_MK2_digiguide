01 - THE BIG PICTURE

The Octatrack's architecture is built on a hierarchy. Understanding this hierarchy is the key to arranging multi-section songs, live jams, and organized sets.

Hierarchy Diagram

KEY CONCEPT

A Part is a complete snapshot of your entire 8-track setup. It stores which samples are on which tracks, which effects you've assigned, all parameter values, and your 16 scenes. When you switch Parts, EVERYTHING changes instantly.

02 - UNDERSTANDING PARTS

This is the most important concept in this tutorial. Most Octatrack confusion comes from misunderstanding Parts.

What is a Part?

THE DEFINITION

A Part is a complete configuration for your 8 tracks. It includes:

  • ✓ Machine type on each track (Static, Flex, Thru, etc.)
  • ✓ Sample assignment on each track
  • ✓ Effect assignments (FX1 + FX2 per track)
  • ✓ All parameter values (filter cutoff, delay send, etc.)
  • ✓ 16 Scenes (crossfader morphs)

How Many Parts Are There?

4 Parts per Bank. 16 Banks total. = 64 Parts in a project.

The Critical Gotcha: Parts are Shared

⚠️ MULTIPLE PATTERNS CAN SHARE ONE PART

In Bank A, you have Patterns A01-A16. Not each pattern has its own Part. Instead, you have only 4 Parts (Part 1, 2, 3, 4) that are shared across all 16 patterns.

Example: Pattern A01, A03, and A07 might all use Part 1. If you change a sample on A01, the change propagates to A03 and A07 automatically because they share the same Part.

03 - SWITCHING PARTS

Parts switch instantly and change your entire 8-track configuration. This is how you create different sections (verse, chorus, bridge) with completely different sounds.

HOW TO SWITCH PARTS

Hold [FUNC] and press one of the 4 Part keys:

[FUNC] + [PART 1/2/3/4]

What Happens When You Switch Parts?

What Changes Effect
Machine assignments on all 8 tracks Tracks instantly load new machine types
Samples on all tracks New samples play (or silence if no sample assigned)
Effect assignments New effects load on each track
All parameter values Filter cutoff, delays, reverbs, everything changes
Scenes 1-16 New scene configuration for crossfader morphs

WHEN TO USE PARTS

Switch Parts when you want a completely different sound palette:

  • ✓ Verse (drums + bass) → Chorus (drums + bass + synth melody)
  • ✓ Intro (just drums) → Main groove (drums + 3 other tracks)
  • ✓ Different effect setups (Part 1 = light FX, Part 2 = heavy FX)
  • ✓ Tempo ramps (switch to Part with different sample BPMs)

04 - BANKS & PATTERN NAVIGATION

The Octatrack has 256 patterns: 16 Banks × 16 Patterns = 256.

Pattern Naming

Bank Patterns Notation
A 1-16 A01, A02, ... A16
B 1-16 B01, B02, ... B16
... ... ...
P 1-16 P01, P02, ... P16

Switching Patterns

SAME BANK (FAST)

Press [TRIG 1-16] to jump to any pattern in the current bank. Quantized to pattern end.

DIFFERENT BANK (SLOWER)

Press [BANK] + bank letter + pattern number:

[BANK] + [B] + [03] → Jump to B03

Also quantized to pattern end.

Organization Tip

Use Banks to organize complete songs or jam setups:

  • Bank A: Song 1 (A01=intro, A02=verse, A03=chorus, etc.)
  • Bank B: Song 2
  • Bank C: Jam templates

05 - SCENES & THE CROSSFADER

Scenes are where you create dynamic, morphing arrangements without changing patterns. The crossfader blends between two scenes, letting you build tension and release.

What is a Scene?

A Scene is a snapshot of parameter values for one side of the crossfader. You have 16 scenes per Part, and you can assign any two of them to the crossfader's A and B buttons.

Setting Up Scenes: Step-by-Step

1

ASSIGN SCENE A

Press [SCENE A], then press any [TRIG 1-16] to assign that scene as your A-side. (e.g., [SCENE A] + [TRIG 1] makes Scene 1 your A side.)

2

ASSIGN SCENE B

Press [SCENE B], then press any [TRIG 1-16] to assign your B-side. (e.g., [SCENE B] + [TRIG 2] makes Scene 2 your B side.)

3

LOCK PARAMETERS TO SCENE A

Hold [SCENE A] and turn any knob (Filter Cutoff, Delay Send, etc.). This "locks" that parameter to Scene A with whatever value you set.

4

LOCK PARAMETERS TO SCENE B

Hold [SCENE B] and turn the same knob to a different value. This locks it to Scene B.

5

USE THE CROSSFADER

Move the crossfader left and right. You're now blending between the two scenes! Left = Scene A values, Right = Scene B values, Middle = interpolated.

WHAT JUST HAPPENED

At the left (Scene A), the Filter Cutoff is open (127) and Delay Send is low (0) — clean sound. At the right (Scene B), the Filter is closed (30) and Delay is high (80) — tension. Moving the crossfader smoothly blends these two states.

06 - BUILDING A TENSION/RELEASE RECIPE

This is a powerful composition tool. Use scenes to dynamically build and release energy without changing patterns or samples.

The Recipe

SCENE A: CLEAN (Release)

  • Filter Cutoff: 127 (fully open)
  • Reverb Send: 10-20 (subtle)
  • Delay Send: 0 (off)
  • All tracks audible (XVOL = 127)

SCENE B: TENSION (Build)

  • Filter Cutoff: 20-40 (closed, darker)
  • Reverb Send: 60-80 (lush, ambient)
  • Delay Send: 60-80 (rhythmic, spacious)
  • Optional: Mute some tracks (XVOL = 0) for contrast

How to Use This in Your Jam

  1. Set up Scene A and Scene B as above
  2. Assign them to crossfader: [SCENE A] + [TRIG 1], [SCENE B] + [TRIG 2]
  3. Start at Scene A (left on crossfader) for your intro — clean and simple
  4. Slowly drag the crossfader right over 4-8 bars — tension builds
  5. At Scene B (right), the track sounds filtered, reverb-heavy, and spacious
  6. Drag back to Scene A to release the tension — clean open sound returns

✓ THE POWER OF THIS APPROACH

You're not changing patterns, samples, or tempo. You're using the same loop, just morphing the timbre and character. Your audience hears a massive energy shift, but technically you're playing the same beat the whole time.

07 - SCENE LOCKS OVERRIDE P-LOCKS

This is important if you're using both per-step parameter locks (p-locks) AND scene locks.

THE RULE

If a step has a p-lock on a parameter AND that parameter is locked to a scene, the SCENE value wins. The crossfader always has priority.

Example

Scenario: You have a drum loop. On step 4, you p-locked the Filter Cutoff to 50. But your scene B has Filter Cutoff locked to 20.

  • When crossfader is at Scene A: p-lock applies (50)
  • When crossfader is at Scene B: Scene B value applies (20), p-lock is ignored
  • When crossfader is in between: interpolated value between the two

HOW TO THINK ABOUT IT

P-locks add detail and rhythmic movement. Scenes are the big, sweeping energy changes. Scenes are the "master controller" — they override the details.

08 - PRACTICAL: MULTI-SECTION JAM SETUP

Let's design a complete multi-section arrangement using Parts, Scenes, and what you've learned.

THE GOAL

Create a song with Verse, Chorus, and Bridge sections, each with distinct sounds and dynamics.

Part Setup

Section Part Track 1 Track 2 Track 3 Notes
Verse Part 1 Drums Bass - Clean, minimal
Chorus Part 2 Drums Bass Synth Melody Add synth, same drums/bass
Bridge Part 3 Drums Bassline (chop) Riser FX Chop the bass, add texture

Scene Setup (Per Part)

For EACH Part, set up the Tension/Release recipe:

SCENE A: Clean (Filter open, FX low) SCENE B: Tension (Filter closed, FX high) This gives you dynamic control WITHIN each part. You get control at TWO levels: 1. Part switches → different samples/machines 2. Scene morphs → different timbre/space

Live Performance Flow

1

START AT PART 1, SCENE A

Press [FUNC] + [PART 1]. Crossfader at left. Clean verse drums + bass.

2

BUILD TENSION IN VERSE

Over 8 bars, drag crossfader right. Same samples, but filtering closes and reverb builds.

3

SWITCH TO CHORUS

Press [FUNC] + [PART 2] (quantized to pattern end). Synth melody appears. Crossfader resets to left (clean again).

4

BUILD TENSION IN CHORUS

Drag crossfader right again. Same scenario — but now with the extra synth layer on top.

5

SWITCH TO BRIDGE

Press [FUNC] + [PART 3]. Bass chop appears, riser FX loads. Crossfader at left again.

6

BACK TO VERSE

Switch back to Part 1, drag crossfader left to release. Synth gone, back to minimal drums + bass.

✓ WHAT YOU'VE CREATED

A multi-section arrangement with dynamic control at every level. You can perform this live, making real-time crossfader movements, switching parts at key moments. It's arranging AND performance in one.
