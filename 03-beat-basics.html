Grid Recording Mode

Grid Recording is how you build patterns on the Octatrack. When you press [REC] , the red LED lights up and you enter a mode where every trig key press (1–16) places or removes a trigger on that step.

Think of it like this: your 16 trig keys correspond to 16 steps in time. Press a key to add a trig, press again to remove it. It's instant visual feedback.

Why Grid Recording? Because it lets you think in steps, not time. You don't have to wait for the pattern to loop to place triggers—you can edit the sequence in real-time.

Key concept: Each trig key fires a sample at that step when the pattern plays. If you load different samples on different tracks and place trigs in the right pattern, you get a beat.

Step Length & Pattern Length

Default: 16 steps (1 bar at 4/4 time)

Longer patterns: Press [FUNC] + [PAGE] to extend your pattern to 32, 48, or 64 steps. More space = more room for arrangement.

Scale per track: Each track can have a different length, creating polyrhythms. For example:

  • Track 1 (Kick): 16 steps
  • Track 2 (Snare): 16 steps
  • Track 3 (Hi-Hat): 32 steps (plays 2x the pattern length for variation)

SCALE menu: Hold [TRIG] on a track, press [FUNC] to open SCALE. Adjust TRK SCALE to change how many steps that track contains. This creates rhythmic complexity without changing overall pattern length.

The Mixer

Press [FUNC] + [LEVEL] to open the mixer. You get 8 track levels + a master level.

What you can control:

  • LEVEL: Volume for each track (0–127)
  • PAN: Stereo position (L–R)
  • BAL: Balance between left/right channels
  • CUE: Send to headphone output for previewing
  • MASTER: Overall output level

The mixer is a quick way to balance your mix without diving into track FX. You can also p-lock mixer values to change them per step.

Playback Controls

[PLAY]: Starts the pattern from step 1. If already playing, it does nothing—press [STOP] first.

[STOP]: Stops playback. Press once to stop at current step, press again to return to step 1.

[TEMPO]: Hold [TEMPO] and turn the encoder to change BPM. Screen shows new tempo in real-time. Range: 30–300 BPM.

Pattern looping: When you press [PLAY], your pattern loops infinitely until you press [STOP]. No manual restart needed—the OT handles it.

Pro tip: Jump to different patterns mid-play. Press [TRIG] 1–16 to switch patterns in same bank (quantized to pattern end). Press [BANK] + letter + number to change banks.

Saving Your Work

PROJECT > SAVE: Saves your working state to a permanent checkpoint. Do this frequently.

SAVE TO NEW: Creates a duplicate project with new name. Safe for experimentation—revert if you mess up.

Important: .work is live working state, auto-saved constantly. .strd is "committed" version. Power off without SAVE = lose .work but keep .strd.

Best practice: After each session, press PROJECT > SAVE. Before risky edits, press PROJECT > SAVE TO NEW for safety.

Warning: Never delete .work or .strd files unless 100% sure you don't need them.
