SAMPLE LOCKS
⏱ 25 MIN
⚡ BEGINNER/INT

1. WHAT ARE SAMPLE LOCKS?

Sample locks let you play a DIFFERENT sample on each step, all on the same track. In [REC] mode, hold any [TRIG] key and browse to assign a unique sample to that step - that's sample locks.

This is the Octatrack's secret weapon for:

  • Building complete drum kits on one track
  • Creating variety and layers without using multiple tracks
  • DJ-style sample juggling
  • Polyrhythmic textures from different samples
KEY CONCEPT
One track, 16 [TRIG] keys, 16 different samples. Each step can play a completely different sound. A red flashing [TRIG] LED tells you which steps have sample locks active.

2. HOW TO SET SAMPLE LOCKS

Here's the exact procedure to lock a sample to a specific step:

STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE
1. Press [REC] to enter GRID RECORDING mode. The sequencer is now ready to record locks.
2. HOLD DOWN one of the [TRIG] keys (1-16) corresponding to the step you want to lock.
3. While holding the [TRIG], press [UP] or [DOWN] to open the sample browser.
4. Navigate to the sample you want and press [YES] to select it.
5. Release the [TRIG] key. The lock is now set.
6. Look at the [TRIG] LED - it will now FLASH RED = sample lock is active on that step.
RED LED = SAMPLE LOCK ACTIVE
When a [TRIG] key has a red flashing LED, it means a sample lock is programmed on that step. This is your visual feedback that something is locked to this trigger. Press [NO] to remove a sample lock from a held [TRIG].

Practice with 2-3 steps first. Once you get the feel for it, you can quickly lock samples across an entire pattern. Press [PLAY] to hear your locks in action, and [STOP] to pause.

3. BUILDING A DRUM KIT ON ONE TRACK

Here's an interactive example of what a drum kit looks like using sample locks. On the Octatrack, you would select a track with [TRACK], enter [REC] mode, and assign samples by holding each [TRIG] key + pressing [UP]/[DOWN]:

DRUM KIT LAYOUT (16 STEPS)
HOW THIS WORKS
Step 1, 5, 9, 13: KICK (deep bass hit)
Step 5, 13 (also): SNARE layered with kick (two different sample locks per track if needed, or on adjacent track)
Step 3, 7, 11, 15: HI-HAT (crisp, high frequency)
Step 4, 12: CLAP (snappy transient)
This pattern creates a classic drum groove all from one track!

Why this is powerful:

  • Uses only 1 track slot (leaves 7 others free for melodies, effects, resampling)
  • Each drum element can be different samples (kick_punchy.wav, snare_tight.wav, etc.)
  • Each step is independent - hold [FUNC] + [TRIG] to mute, or hold a [TRIG] and tweak parameters to p-lock individual hits
  • Real-time adjustments: swap samples without re-locking

4. SAMPLE LOCKS + P-LOCKS = POWER

Each sample-locked step can ALSO have parameter locks. In [REC] mode, hold a [TRIG] key and adjust any parameter with the encoders to p-lock that value to that step. This means infinite variation:

LOCK TYPE EFFECT EXAMPLE
Sample Lock Different sample per step Step 1 = kick, Step 3 = snare
Sample Lock + Volume P-Lock Different volume per sample Hold [TRIG], adjust [LEVEL] — Step 1 kick at 100, Step 5 kick at 80
Sample Lock + Pitch P-Lock Different pitch per sample Hold [TRIG], press [PLAYBACK] page, tune pitch per step
Sample Lock + Filter P-Lock Different filter per sample Hold [TRIG], press [FX1], tweak filter — closed on step 4, open on step 12
Sample Lock + Effect Send Different effect amount per step Hold [TRIG], press [FX2], dial reverb — dry kick on step 1, wet snare on step 5
REAL EXAMPLE
Track 3, Step 1: kick.wav with no filter
Track 3, Step 5: snare.wav with 50% reverb send
Track 3, Step 9: kick.wav pitched up +3 semitones
Track 3, Step 13: hat.wav at 70% volume
Result: one track, unlimited texture and color

Use [PAGE] to navigate between [PLAYBACK], [AMP], [LFO], [FX1], and [FX2] pages while holding a [TRIG] to p-lock any parameter. This combination is what separates basic sequencing from expressive Octatrack performance.

5. SAMPLE LOCKS vs SLICING: WHICH TO USE?

Both are powerful techniques. Here's when to use each:

FEATURE SAMPLE LOCKS SLICING
Source Multiple different samples One sample divided
Slots used 1 slot per unique sample 1 slot total
Best for Drum kits, variety, different sounds Chopping loops, beat rearrangement
RAM cost Each sample (Flex) = memory One sample only = minimal
Flexibility Totally different sounds per step Variations of the same source
Setup speed Slower (hold [TRIG] + [UP]/[DOWN] per step) Faster (open [AED], create slice grid once)
Real-time swaps Yes! Instant sample changes Fixed to sample slices
DECISION TREE
Are you chopping a single drum loop into pieces? → SLICING (use [AED] to set slices)
Building a kit from separate kicks, snares, hats? → SAMPLE LOCKS (hold [TRIG] + [UP]/[DOWN])
Want to swap samples during a live jam? → SAMPLE LOCKS
Want to remix a single loop in new ways? → SLICING

6. PERFORMANCE TIPS & TRICKS

Master these techniques for faster, cleaner sample locking:

PRE-BROWSING
Before starting a jam, hold a [TRIG] + press [UP]/[DOWN] to pre-browse samples. This keeps them in the "recent" section of the browser, so you don't have to dig through folders during a live performance.
MACHINE COMPATIBILITY
Sample locks work with ANY machine type: Flex, Static, Neighbor, Thru, Pickup. You're not limited to one type!
CROSS-FOLDER SAMPLES
You can sample-lock samples from DIFFERENT folders. No need to organize everything into one place - flexibility is part of the design.
COMBINE WITH TRIG CONDITIONS
Use FILL trig conditions with sample locks for instant fill variations. Example: Normal pattern uses kick, but when holding [YES], a different kick sample plays (FILL condition).
VISUAL FEEDBACK
Red flashing [TRIG] LED = your lock is active. When you press [PLAY], watch for those flashes to confirm which steps are locked. Press [FUNC] + [REC] to view all active locks. Very useful during jams.

Pro move: Lock a series of break-heavy samples to the first half of a pattern, then sparse ambient samples to the second half. Your jam automatically evolves.

7. LIVE LOOP SWITCHING: DJ STYLE

Use sample locks to switch between different loop sections in real time - like a DJ cutting between breaks:

SETUP
1. Load a long sample (e.g., 8-bar loop) and open [AED] to slice it
2. Slice it into sections (first 2 bars, next 2 bars, etc.)
3. Enter [REC] mode and sample-lock each section to different [TRIG] steps
4. Add ONE-SHOT trigs ([FUNC] + [TRIG]) to each step to prevent re-triggering
5. Press [PLAY] — now each [TRIG] press jumps to a different section

This is powerful for:

  • Breaking long tracks into loops to jam with
  • Remixing live - play sections in random order
  • Building tension by repeating a climactic section
  • DJ-style transitions between tracks
EXAMPLE JAM SCENARIO
Step 1: Dark intro section (first 2 bars)
Step 2: Builds to pre-chorus (next 2 bars)
Step 3: Peak moment (next 2 bars)
Step 4: Drop / breakdown
Now during your jam, press [TRIG] 3 repeatedly to hammer the peak, then drop to [TRIG] 4 for impact. Use [SCENE A] and [SCENE B] on the [CROSSFADER] for even more control!
