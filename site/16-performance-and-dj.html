Scenes & Crossfader Deep Dive

In Tutorial 08 you learned that scenes exist. Now you will learn to weaponize them. Scenes are the core of live performance on the Octatrack — they let you morph between completely different sound states with a single fader movement. Every Part holds 16 scenes , and you assign any two of them to the crossfader's A and B endpoints.

Assigning Scenes to the Crossfader

The crossfader has two slots: Scene A (left endpoint) and Scene B (right endpoint). To assign a scene to either slot:

1

Assign Scene A

Press [SCENE A] + a [TRIG] key (1–16). The chosen scene is now assigned to the A slot (left endpoint). The currently assigned scene is shown by a red [TRIG] LED. Scenes containing locks appear as half-bright green LEDs.

[SCENE A] hold + [TRIG 1]
2

Assign Scene B

Press [SCENE B] + a [TRIG] key. This scene sits at the right endpoint of the crossfader.

[SCENE B] hold + [TRIG 9]
3

Lock Parameters to a Scene

Hold a [SCENE A] or [SCENE B] key, then turn any DATA ENTRY knob to lock that parameter value to the scene. The parameter graphics invert to show the locked value. To remove a lock, hold the same [SCENE A] or [SCENE B] key and press the corresponding DATA ENTRY knob.

[SCENE B] hold + turn [LEVEL]

Manual reference: Section 10.3 — Scenes (page 53). Scene volume locking in Section 10.3.2 (page 54).

How the Crossfader Interpolates

The crossfader does not simply switch between two states. It interpolates between the locked scene parameter values smoothly. With the fader fully left, Scene A's locks are in full effect. Fully right, Scene B takes over. In between, the Octatrack calculates intermediate values for every locked parameter.

If only one scene has locks, the crossfader interpolates between that scene's values and the default Part parameter values . This is how "fade from default to scene" works.

Priority rule: Scene locks OVERRIDE parameter locks (p-locks). When the crossfader is active, the scene always wins. This ensures smooth transitions without sudden jumps from p-locked steps. (Manual Section 10.3, page 53)

XVOL: Equal-Energy Crossfading

When you crossfade between two tracks using regular volume, there is a volume dip at the center position . This is because linear fading drops both sides to 50% at the midpoint (0.5 + 0.5 = perceived quieter than 1.0).

The Octatrack solves this with XVOL — a special scene-only parameter found on the [AMP] page. XVOL fades volume in an equal-energy fashion , preventing the center dip entirely.

Parameter Found On MIN Setting MAX Setting
XLV [LEVEL] knob (hold [SCENE A] or [SCENE B]) Mutes signal post-FX Full level
XVOL [AMP] page (hold [SCENE A] or [SCENE B]) Mutes signal pre-FX Full volume
XDIR AB [MIX] page knob B (hold [SCENE A] or [SCENE B]) Mutes input AB Full DIR level
XDIR CD [MIX] page knob E (hold [SCENE A] or [SCENE B]) Mutes input CD Full DIR level

To set XVOL: hold [SCENE A] or [SCENE B], navigate to the [AMP] page, and the VOL parameter changes to XVOL. Set it to MIN on the scene you want silent, MAX on the scene you want audible.

Scene Muting

You can instantly disable a scene without moving the crossfader by pressing [FUNC] + [SCENE A] or [FUNC] + [SCENE B]. When a scene is muted, its locked parameters are ignored and the Part's default values take effect. This is useful for creating sudden transitions — snap from a heavily processed scene back to clean defaults.

Tension & Release

The core technique of Octatrack performance is tension and release . You design two opposing sound states and use the crossfader to move between them. Scene A is your "home base" (clean, open, full), and Scene B is your "tension zone" (filtered, effected, stripped down). Moving the crossfader builds tension; snapping it back delivers the drop.

Recipe 1: Filter Sweep Build

Drums: Clean to Filtered

Scene A (clean):

  • No locks needed — Part defaults are your clean state
  • Or lock filters fully open: [FX1] FREQ = 127

Scene B (tension):

  1. Select the drum track. Press [FX1] to open the effects page.
  2. Hold [SCENE B] and turn the FREQ knob down to ~30. The display inverts to show the lock.
  3. Hold [SCENE B] and turn the RESO (resonance) knob up to ~80 for a resonant sweep.
  4. Hold [SCENE B], press [FX2], and lock DELAY SEND to ~80 for echo buildup.

Result: Moving the crossfader right gradually closes the filter and adds delay. Snap it left for the clean drop.

Recipe 2: Breakdown Builder

Full Mix to Bass + Kick Only

Scene A: All tracks at full volume (default).

Scene B setup:

  1. Select each track you want to mute during breakdown (hats, pads, leads).
  2. For each: hold [SCENE B], press [AMP], and set XVOL to MIN.
  3. Leave kick and bass tracks untouched (or lock their XVOL to MAX).

Result: Crossfade right = only kick and bass remain. Crossfade left = full mix returns. The XVOL parameter ensures equal-energy fading with no volume dip.

[SCENE B] hold + [AMP] set XVOL = MIN

Recipe 3: Reverb Wash

Dry to Ambient

Scene A: All reverb sends at 0 (dry).

Scene B:

  1. For each track: hold [SCENE B], open [FX2] (assign Dark Reverb if not set).
  2. Lock reverb MIX (dry/wet) to ~100.
  3. Lock DTIME (decay) to ~90 for a long tail.
  4. Optionally lock the filter on [FX1] to close slightly for a muffled wash.

Result: Crossfade right = everything drowns in reverb. Great for transitions between sections.

Scene stacking technique (from Merlin's guide): Build scenes incrementally. Scene 1 = slight filter. Copy Scene 1 to Scene 9 (hold [SCENE A] + [FUNC] + [REC] to copy, then hold [SCENE B] + [FUNC] + [STOP] to paste). Add more changes to Scene 9. Copy to Scene 2. Add more. This way each scene "stacks" on the previous, creating a natural progression. Scenes 1–8 go on the [SCENE A] slot, 9–16 on [SCENE B]. Fade between them in sequence for evolving sets.

CUE Output Mastery

The CUE output is your secret weapon. It is a separate stereo output routed to headphones, completely independent of what the audience hears on the MAIN outputs. Think of it as a DJ's headphone pre-listen system built directly into the Octatrack.

Cueing Tracks for Preview

1

Cue a track

Press [CUE] + [TRACK] key (e.g. [T3]) to route that track to the CUE output. The [TRACK] LED flashes to indicate it is cued. The track remains audible on MAIN as well.

[CUE] + [T3]
2

Cue an input

Press [CUE] + [REC1] or [CUE] + [REC2] to cue the audio inputs (AB or CD). Useful for previewing an external DJ deck or synth before mixing it in.

[CUE] + [REC2]
3

Adjust headphone mix

Press [MIX] to open the MIXER menu. The MIX parameter controls the headphone balance: fully left = mirror of MAIN outputs, fully right = only CUE audio. Set it somewhere in between to hear both.

[MIX] adjust MIX
4

Set CUE level per track

Hold [CUE] and turn the [LEVEL] encoder to set the CUE output level independently from the MAIN level. Each track has its own CUE level.

[CUE] hold + turn [LEVEL]

CUE as an FX Send Bus

One of the Octatrack's most creative routing tricks: use the CUE output as a send bus . Route tracks to CUE, then record the CUE mix via SRC3 = CUE on a recorder track. This lets you capture a processed submix without affecting the MAIN output.

1

Cue the tracks you want to process: [CUE] + [T1], [CUE] + [T2], etc.

2

On a recorder track, press [SRC] and set SRC3 to CUE . Set SRC1/SRC2 to - .

3

The recorder now captures only the cued tracks. Apply FX on those tracks and resample the result.

Limitation: CUE output is NOT available on Track 8 when it is set as a master track. Also, if the CUE MUTES TRACK option is enabled in PERSONALIZE menu, cueing a track will also mute it from MAIN — useful for silent preview but changes behavior. (Manual Section 8.5, page 32)

Thru Machines & Master Bus

Thru machines listen to the Octatrack's audio inputs in real time and pass them through the track's effect chain. This turns the Octatrack into a live audio processor for external gear, DJ decks, or even its own output.

The Master Bus Trick

The most common Thru machine technique: use Track 8 as a master bus to apply compression and EQ to the entire mix.

1

Enable Master Track

Go to PROJECT > CONTROL > AUDIO > MASTER. Set it to active. Track 8 now processes all other tracks' output.

2

Assign effects

Select [T8]. Press [FX1] — assign a Compressor . Press [FX2] — assign DJ Kill EQ or 2-Band Parametric EQ .

[T8] → [FX1] = Compressor [FX2] = DJ Kill EQ
3

Result

All audio from Tracks 1–7 now passes through [T8]'s compressor and EQ before reaching the MAIN output. The master track is immune to mutes — it always passes audio.

Manual reference: Section 8.6.1 — MASTER setting (page 35). Master track effects in Section 11 (page 57).

DJ Setup: Two Thru Machines

For DJ mixing, connect two decks to the Octatrack's input pairs and use Thru machines to process each independently. This is the setup from Manual Section 16.4.2:

1

Connect decks

DJ Deck 1 to Input pair AB. DJ Deck 2 to Input pair CD.

2

Set DIR to 0

Press [MIX] and set DIR for both AB and CD to 0. This routes incoming audio exclusively to Thru machines (not directly to MAIN).

[MIX] DIR AB = 0, DIR CD = 0
3

Assign Thru machines

On [T1]: assign a Thru machine. Press [SRC] and set INAB to A B . On [T5]: assign a Thru machine and set INCD to C D .

[T1] Thru → [SRC] INAB = A B
[T5] Thru → [SRC] INCD = C D
4

Set up crossfader for mixing

Assign Scene 1 to [SCENE A] and Scene 2 to [SCENE B]. On Track 1: hold [SCENE A] and set XLV to MAX. Hold [SCENE B] and set XLV to MIN. On Track 5: reverse it — hold [SCENE A] and set XLV to MIN, hold [SCENE B] and set XLV to MAX.

5

Place trigs and play

Place a trig on step 1 for both [T1] and [T5]. Press [PLAY]. Thru machines need at least one trig to start passing audio.

[REC] → [TRIG 1] (on [T1] and [T5]) → [PLAY]
6

Preview and mix

Cue Deck 2: press [CUE] + [T5]. Beat-match in headphones. Un-cue and move the crossfader to blend. Because these are Thru machines, you can apply [FX1] and [FX2] to each deck independently — filter sweeps, delays, lo-fi effects.

[CUE] + [T5] preview deck 2

Pro tip: For tempo-synced effects like Echo Freeze, tap the [TEMPO] button in time with the playing deck. You can also sample the DJ decks while mixing — even the cued one. (Manual page 101)

Quick Mutes & Track Control

Muting and unmuting tracks is one of the simplest but most effective performance techniques. The Octatrack offers several ways to control what the audience hears in real time.

Direct Track Muting

The fastest method: press [FUNC] + [TRACK] key to instantly mute or unmute any audio track.

[FUNC] + [T1] mute/unmute Track 1
LED State Meaning
Yellow [TRACK] key (active track) Track is muted and currently selected
Unlit [TRACK] key Track is muted and not selected
Normal brightness Track is audible

MIXER Menu Mutes

Press [MIX] to open the MIXER menu, then use [TRIG] keys 1–8 to mute/unmute audio tracks (and [TRIG] 9–16 for MIDI tracks). This gives you a clear visual overview of all mute states at once.

Queued mutes: Hold [FUNC] and press [TRIG] keys to queue mute changes. The mutes will not take effect until you release [FUNC] — perfect for coordinated mute/unmute of multiple tracks simultaneously.

[MIX] → [FUNC] hold + [TRIG 1] [TRIG 3] [TRIG 5] release [FUNC] = all three toggle at once

Solo Mode

In the MIXER menu, press [CUE] + [TRIG] key to solo a track. Only the soloed track plays; all others are temporarily muted. Press again to exit solo. Tracks that were muted before solo will remain muted when you exit.

[MIX] → [CUE] + [TRIG 2] solo Track 2

Key behaviors to remember:

  • Muting a track stops its audio, but effects tails continue (reverb/delay decay naturally).
  • If [T8] is set as a master track, it is immune to mutes .
  • Muting the tracks preceding a Neighbor machine chain has no effect. Mute the last Neighbor in the chain to silence it.
  • If a track is cued, muting it removes it from MAIN but it remains audible on CUE outputs.

XVOL Muting via Crossfader

For more musical muting, use XVOL in scenes (covered in Section 1). Lock XVOL to MIN on [SCENE B] for tracks you want to fade out. The crossfader then smoothly removes them rather than cutting abruptly. This is preferable for ambient, dub, and melodic sets.

The Drop technique: Mute everything except kick for 2–4 bars. Queue all unmutes with [FUNC] hold in the [MIX] menu. Release [FUNC] right on the downbeat for a coordinated full-mix return. Practice the timing — this is one of the most crowd-pleasing moves.

Pattern Transitions Live

Switching patterns is how you move through sections of a set. The Octatrack quantizes pattern changes to the end of the current pattern by default, so you always land on the downbeat.

Same Bank

Simply press a [TRIG] key (1–16) while in normal mode (not [REC]). The new pattern queues and starts when the current pattern finishes its cycle.

[TRIG 5] Pattern 5 queued, starts at end of current pattern

Different Bank

Press [BANK] + a [TRIG] key to select the bank (A–P), then press a [TRIG] key to select the pattern within that bank. You must complete the selection within 4 seconds.

[BANK] + [TRIG 2] Bank B → [TRIG 1] Pattern B1

The Crossfader Transition Trick

The most important live technique on the Octatrack. It lets you switch patterns with zero audio gaps :

1

Set up a recorder track

Assign a Flex machine to a track (e.g. [T7]). Set up its recorder: press [SRC] and set SRC3 = MAIN. Set RLEN to match your pattern length.

2

Record the current mix

Arm the recorder and capture a loop of the currently playing pattern. Place a one-shot recorder trig so it captures exactly one pattern cycle.

[T7] → [FUNC] + [TRIG 1] (one-shot recorder trig)
3

Crossfade to the recording

Set up scenes so the crossfader fades from the live mix to the recorded loop on [T7]. Use XLV on the recording track: [SCENE A] = MIN, [SCENE B] = MAX. On all other tracks: [SCENE A] = MAX, [SCENE B] = MIN.

4

Switch patterns while crossfaded

With the crossfader fully on the recording ([SCENE B]), the audience hears the looped recording. Now change the pattern. The new pattern loads silently because its tracks are crossfaded out.

5

Crossfade back

Move the crossfader back to [SCENE A]. The new pattern fades in seamlessly while the recording fades out.

Part switching is dangerous: Changing Parts replaces ALL machine assignments, samples, FX, and scenes. Unlike pattern changes, Part changes are not seamless — the audio will change instantly. Use Part changes deliberately between songs, not during a transition. If you need variety within a song, use different patterns within the same Part.

Building a Live Set

This is where everything comes together. You will build a 4-pattern performance set from scratch, combining scenes, CUE preview, master bus processing, mutes, and transitions.

Step-by-Step: Your First Performance Set

1

Create 4 patterns in Bank A

Pattern A1 = Intro (minimal: kick + pad). Pattern A2 = Verse (add bass, hats). Pattern A3 = Chorus (full energy, all tracks). Pattern A4 = Breakdown (stripped back, FX-heavy).

All four patterns should share the same Part so your machines, effects, and scenes carry across.

[BANK] + [TRIG 1] Bank A
2

Design Scene A (clean/home)

Assign Scene 1 to [SCENE A] by pressing [SCENE A] + [TRIG 1]. This scene has no locks — it represents your default Part settings. The clean state. Optionally lock filters fully open and reverb sends to 0 for safety.

[SCENE A] + [TRIG 1]
3

Design Scene B (tension)

Assign Scene 9 to [SCENE B] by pressing [SCENE B] + [TRIG 9]. Now build the tension state:

  • Hold [SCENE B], open [FX1], lock FREQ down to 40 on drum track (filter close)
  • Hold [SCENE B], open [FX2], lock reverb MIX to 80 (wash)
  • Hold [SCENE B], open [AMP], set XVOL = MIN on hi-hat and pad tracks (mute via crossfader)
[SCENE B] + [TRIG 9]
4

Set up CUE for headphone preview

Press [MIX]. Set the MIX parameter to center (50/50 MAIN and CUE in headphones). Now you can cue any track with [CUE] + [TRACK] to preview it before it goes live.

[MIX] MIX = center
5

Set up [T8] as master bus

Go to PROJECT > CONTROL > AUDIO > MASTER = ON. Select [T8]. Assign [FX1] = Compressor (set THRS around 64, RATIO around 4:1, ATK fast, REL medium). Assign [FX2] = DJ Kill EQ (leave flat for now — use in performance to cut frequencies).

[T8] → [FX1] = Compressor → [FX2] = DJ Kill EQ
6

Practice: Perform through all 4 patterns

  1. Press [PLAY]. Start on Pattern A1 (Intro). Crossfader left (clean).
  2. Build tension: slowly move crossfader right during A1.
  3. Queue Pattern A2 by pressing [TRIG 2]. It starts on the next cycle.
  4. Snap crossfader left as A2 kicks in — full energy verse.
  5. Use [FUNC] + [T3] to mute hats for 2 bars, then unmute on the downbeat.
  6. Queue A3 (Chorus). Move crossfader to center for subtle FX layering.
  7. For the breakdown: queue A4, crossfade right to tension, mute drums with [FUNC] + [T1].
  8. Bring everything back: unmute, snap crossfader left, queue A1 to loop back to the intro.
[PLAY] → [TRIG 2] (queue) → [FUNC] + [T3] (mute)
7

Record your performance with the Arranger

Once you have the transitions down, use the Arranger (Tutorial 13) to sequence the 4 patterns into a fixed arrangement. Set pattern lengths and scene assignments per row. Press [PLAY] in Arranger mode and the Octatrack performs the set automatically while you focus on crossfader and mutes.
