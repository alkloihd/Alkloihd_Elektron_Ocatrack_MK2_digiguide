FX & Neighbors: Effects Chains

Master the dual-FX system, build Neighbor chains, process external audio, and create a master bus.

What You Will Learn

  • How the dual [FX1]/[FX2] effect slots work on every audio track
  • Every effect category available and when to use each one
  • How to chain tracks with Neighbor machines for 4+ effects
  • Processing external audio with Thru machines
  • The master bus trick (Track 8 as mix bus processor)
  • Using [CUE] output as a send/return effects bus
Prerequisites: 02 - Load & Lock , 03 - Beat Basics , 08 - Parts, Banks & Scenes

FX Architecture Overview

Every one of the 8 audio tracks on the Octatrack has two effect slots : [FX1] and [FX2]. These are applied in series -- audio flows through [FX1] first, then [FX2], then out to the mixer.

To access FX parameters: select a track with [TRACK], then press [FX1] or [FX2]. The six encoders control the effect's main parameters. Press [FUNC] + [FX1] or [FUNC] + [FX2] (or quickly double-press [FX1] / [FX2]) to open the SETUP menu where you assign which effect is loaded into that slot.

Key Concept

Effects are stored per-Part , not per-pattern. If two patterns share the same Part, they share the same [FX1] and [FX2] assignments. Change the effect on one, and it changes on the other.

Signal Flow: Single Track

Notice that [FX2] has access to all the effects that [FX1] has, plus four additional time-based/reverb effects: Echo Freeze Delay, Gatebox Plate Reverb, Spring Reverb, and Dark Reverb. This is why the typical approach is to put filters/dynamics on [FX1] and delays/reverbs on [FX2].

Pro Tip

Press [FX1] + [RECORD] to copy FX1 settings. Then select another track and press [FX1] + [STOP] to paste . Works for [FX2] too. Fast way to duplicate your effect setup across tracks.

FX1/FX2 Categories

Below is the complete catalog of effects available. The "Slot" column shows whether the effect is available in [FX1], [FX2], or both.

Category Effect Slot What It Does
Filter 12/24dB Multi Mode Filter FX1+FX2 LP/HP/BP/Notch with resonance. BASE + WIDTH controls. Sweep for classic filter effects. Includes filter envelope with ATK/DEC.
Comb Filter FX1+FX2 Metallic resonant overtones. Pitch-tuned feedback for robotic / tuned percussion textures.
EQ 2-Band Parametric EQ FX1+FX2 Two bands with frequency, gain, and Q (bandwidth). Choose shelf or parametric mode per band.
DJ Kill EQ FX1+FX2 3-band EQ that can completely kill each band. Low/Mid/Hi gain from +12dB boost to full kill.
Delay Echo Freeze Delay FX2 Only Tempo-synced delay with feedback. LOCK mode turns it into a repeat/stutter/freeze effect. Ping-pong option.
Reverb Dark Reverb FX2 Only Lush, wide stereo reverb with long decay. Pre-delay control. Best for ambient / spacey sounds.
Gatebox Plate Reverb FX2 Only Plate reverb with gating. Gate time and threshold let you create gated reverb snare effects.
Spring Reverb FX2 Only Classic spring reverb emulation. Grungy, twangy character. Three spring types from transparent to splashy.
Modulation 2-10 Stage Phaser FX1+FX2 Sweeping phase effect. Variable number of stages. Stereo width control.
Flanger FX1+FX2 Classic flanging with depth, speed, and feedback. Evenly-spaced notches in frequency spectrum.
2-10 Tap Chorus FX1+FX2 Variable tap stereo chorus. Softens and widens. Great for pads and synth processing.
Dynamics Dynamix Compressor FX1+FX2 Full compressor: attack, release, threshold, ratio, gain. RMS/peak detection. Parallel compression via MIX.
Lo-Fi Lo-Fi Collection FX1+FX2 Distortion + sample rate reduction + bit crushing + amplitude modulation. All-in-one destruction unit.
Spatial Spatializer FX1+FX2 Stereo image control. Widen, narrow, or create pseudo-stereo from mono. Mid/Side processing.
Important

Echo Freeze Delay and all three reverbs (Dark, Plate, Spring) are FX2-only . Plan your signal chain accordingly: put filters, compression, or EQ on [FX1], then time-based effects on [FX2]. Need delay AND reverb? That is where Neighbor machines come in.

Assigning Effects

There are two ways to assign an effect to a slot:

  1. Press [FUNC] + [FX1] (or [FUNC] + [FX2]) to open the setup menu directly
  2. Quickly double-press [FX1] (or [FX2]) to open the same setup menu

In the setup menu, use [ARROW] keys ([LEFT]/[RIGHT]/[UP]/[DOWN]) to browse the effects list on the left. Press [YES] to assign. The setup parameters appear on the right side.

[FUNC] + [FX1] → browse list → [YES] to assign

Neighbor Machines

Two effects per track is powerful, but sometimes you need more. That is what Neighbor machines are for. A track assigned as a Neighbor receives the audio output from the immediately preceding track and applies its own [FX1] + [FX2] on top.

This lets you chain effects beyond the 2-slot limit:

Neighbor Chain: 4 Tracks = 8 Effects

Neighbor Rules

  • Cannot be on [T1] or [T5] -- there is no preceding track to listen to (tracks are grouped 1-4 and 5-8)
  • A Neighbor receives audio from the immediately preceding track only
  • Chain up to 4 tracks: [T1] → [T2] → [T3] → [T4] or [T5] → [T6] → [T7] → [T8] = 8 simultaneous effects
  • Neighbor machines use zero RAM -- no sample is loaded, they just pass audio
  • The Neighbor machine has no SRC parameters -- it automatically takes the previous track's output
Muting Gotcha

If you use Neighbor machines, you cannot mute the tracks preceding the last Neighbor in the chain. To silence a Neighbor chain, mute the last Neighbor track in the chain. For example, in a T1 → T2 → T3 chain, press [FUNC] + [T3] to mute -- pressing [FUNC] + [T1] or [FUNC] + [T2] will have no effect.

Setting Up a Neighbor

1

Select the target track

Press [TRACK] to select the track you want as a Neighbor (must be [T2], [T3], [T4], [T6], [T7], or [T8]).

[TRACK] → [T2]
2

Assign Neighbor machine

Double-press [TRACK] to open QUICK ASSIGN. Select NEIGHBOR from the machine type list and press [YES].

[TRACK] x2 → NEIGHBOR → [YES]
3

Assign effects to the Neighbor track

Press [FUNC] + [FX1] to choose [FX1] effect. Press [FUNC] + [FX2] to choose [FX2] effect.

[FUNC] + [FX1] → choose effect → [YES]
Pro Tip

Even though Neighbor tracks do not need sample trigs to pass audio, you CAN still use their sequencer for trigless trigs with parameter locks . This means you can p-lock [FX1] and [FX2] parameters on Neighbor tracks to create rhythmic effect changes -- like a filter sweep that opens only on beat 3, or delay feedback that increases every 4 bars.

Thru Machines

While Neighbor machines chain internal audio between tracks, Thru machines bring external audio into the Octatrack for processing. Connect a synth, drum machine, guitar, or any audio source to the OT inputs, and a Thru machine lets you apply [FX1] + [FX2] in real time.

Thru Machine Setup

1

Connect external audio

Plug your audio source into input pair A/B (or C/D) on the back of the Octatrack.

2

Set DIR to 0 in [MIX]

Press [MIX] to open the mixer. Set DIR for AB (or CD) to 0. This prevents the raw input from going directly to the main outputs -- you only want to hear it through the Thru machine.

[MIX] → DIR AB = 0
3

Assign Thru machine

Select a track, double-press [TRACK] for QUICK ASSIGN. Choose THRU and press [YES].

[TRACK] x2 → THRU → [YES]
4

Set input source on [SRC] page

Press [SRC]. Set INAB to the input pair your audio is connected to (e.g., A B for both channels of the AB pair).

[SRC] → INAB = A B
5

Place a trig and press [PLAY]

Thru machines must be trigged to start passing audio. Enter [REC] mode and place a trig on step 1 with [TRIG 1]. Press [PLAY] to start the sequencer. Audio flows through.

[REC] → [TRIG 1] → [PLAY]
6

Add effects

Assign [FX1] and [FX2] as usual. Use p-locks, scenes, and LFOs to automate effect parameters on external audio.

[FUNC] + [FX1] → choose effect
DIR vs Thru

The OT has two ways to hear external audio. DIR (in the [MIX] menu) passes audio directly to the outputs -- it bypasses track effects entirely. Thru machines route audio through a track so you can apply [FX1] + [FX2]. If you hear a slightly phased sound, it is because both DIR and the Thru signal are playing with a tiny latency offset. Set DIR to 0 when using Thru machines.

Pro Tip

Combine Thru + Neighbor for massive FX chains on external audio. [T1] = Thru listening to your synth, [T2] = Neighbor with reverb + delay, [T3] = Neighbor with compressor + EQ. Six effects on your external synth, all controllable via scenes and p-locks.

Master Bus Trick

The Octatrack does not have a dedicated master bus processor, but there is a well-known workaround: turn Track 8 into a master track .

How It Works

Go to PROJECT > CONTROL > MASTER and set it to active . This turns [T8] into a master track. All audio from tracks 1-7 passes through [T8]'s effects before reaching the main outputs.

1

Enable Master Track

Press [PROJ], navigate to CONTROL, find MASTER, set to ON. [T8] is now a master track.

[PROJ] → CONTROL → MASTER = ON
2

Assign Compressor to [FX1]

Select [T8], press [FUNC] + [FX1]. Choose Dynamix Compressor. Set a gentle ratio (2:1 to 4:1), moderate attack, and adjust threshold to taste.

[T8] → [FUNC] + [FX1] → COMPRESSOR
3

Assign EQ to [FX2]

Press [FUNC] + [FX2]. Choose DJ Kill EQ or Parametric EQ. Shape your overall mix tone.

[FUNC] + [FX2] → DJ KILL EQ
Trade-off

Enabling the master track means you lose [T8] as a regular track . You now have 7 audio tracks instead of 8. The master track cannot be muted with [FUNC] + [T8]. It only responds to [AMP], [LFO], [FX1], and [FX2] parameter pages.

Pro Tip

The master track works brilliantly with scenes . Set up Scene A with clean EQ and light compression (your normal mix), and Scene B with crushed compression, bass-boosted EQ, and reduced highs (your breakdown/build texture). Crossfade between them for instant mix-wide transitions.

CUE as Send FX

The Octatrack's [CUE] output is normally used for headphone monitoring. But it can also function as a send/return effects bus -- a way to route specific tracks through a shared reverb or delay without dedicating Neighbor machines to each one.

The Concept

The idea: route tracks you want to process to the [CUE] output. Set up a recorder track with SRC3 = CUE to capture just those signals. Play the recording back through a Flex machine with effects applied.

1

Route tracks to CUE

Hold [CUE] and press [TRACK] keys for the tracks you want to send. The track LED changes to indicate cue routing.

[CUE] + [T1] (toggle)
2

Set up a recorder track

Choose an available track (e.g., [T7]). Open Recording Setup with [FUNC] + [REC3]. Set SRC3 to CUE. This captures only the cue-routed audio.

[T7] → [FUNC] + [REC3] → SRC3 = CUE
3

Assign effects to the return track

On [T7], assign a Flex machine with the recorder buffer. Add [FX1] = Dark Reverb and/or [FX2] = Echo Freeze Delay. This is your shared send effect.

Why Use CUE Send?

Neighbor machines are great but they eat tracks. The CUE send trick lets multiple tracks share one reverb without dedicating a Neighbor to each. The trade-off: the [CUE] output goes to headphones, so you cannot preview other tracks while using this method.

Step-by-Step

1

Set up Track 1 with drums

Select [T1]. Double-press [TRACK] to assign a Flex machine. Load a drum loop sample. Enter [REC] mode, place a trig on [TRIG 1].

[T1] → [TRACK] x2 → FLEX → load sample
2

Assign filter to [FX1] on Track 1

With [T1] selected, press [FUNC] + [FX1]. Select "Multi Mode Filter" from the list. Press [YES].

[FUNC] + [FX1] → MULTI MODE FILTER → [YES]
3

Assign delay to [FX2] on Track 1

Press [FUNC] + [FX2]. Select "Echo Freeze Delay". Press [YES].

[FUNC] + [FX2] → ECHO FREEZE DELAY → [YES]
4

Assign Neighbor machine to Track 2

Select [T2]. Double-press [TRACK]. Choose NEIGHBOR. Press [YES]. Track 2 now receives audio from Track 1.

[T2] → [TRACK] x2 → NEIGHBOR → [YES]
5

Assign reverb to [FX1] on Track 2

With [T2] selected, press [FUNC] + [FX1]. Select "Dark Reverb". Press [YES]. Note: Dark Reverb is normally FX2-only, but on a Neighbor track you may prefer Compressor on [FX1] and reverb on [FX2]. Let us swap: put Compressor on [FX1] and Dark Reverb on [FX2].

[FUNC] + [FX1] → COMPRESSOR → [YES]
6

Assign Dark Reverb to [FX2] on Track 2

Press [FUNC] + [FX2]. Select "Dark Reverb". Press [YES].

[FUNC] + [FX2] → DARK REVERB → [YES]
7

Press [PLAY] and adjust

Your drum loop now runs through: Filter → Delay → Compressor → Reverb. Four effects in series. Tweak parameters on each track's [FX1] and [FX2] pages to sculpt your sound.

[PLAY]
Pro Tip

Use [LEVEL] on [T2] to control the overall chain volume post-effects. Since the Neighbor is the last link in the chain, its [LEVEL] is your master volume for the processed signal.

Correct! The delay and all three reverbs are FX2-exclusive. All other effects are available in both slots.

Track 4 and Track 8 (end of each group)
Track 1 only
Track 1 and Track 5 (no preceding track in their group)
Any odd-numbered track (1, 3, 5, 7)
Correct! Neighbor machines listen to the preceding track. Tracks 1 and 5 are the first in their groups (1-4 and 5-8), so there is no preceding track to listen to.

Mute all three tracks individually
Correct! With Neighbor chains, only the last Neighbor track can be muted. Pressing [FUNC] + [T1] or [FUNC] + [T2] will have no effect on the audio.

Correct! Thru machines require a trig to start passing audio through. Place a sample trig on step 1 and start the sequencer.
5. What is the maximum number of effects you can chain on a single audio source using Neighbor machines?
